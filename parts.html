Hydra Prosthetic Arm

The forearm has spots for 2 9v batteries in the back, an arduino uno board, the gear box, actuators and thumb servo. It is printed in 3 parts to get the nice 3d printed look we were going for.

The back of the forearm has a connector for the other half of the servo to allow the wrist connection.

Hydra's hand mechanics is aimed to give the user as much functionality as possible with 3d printed parts. Joints are held together with plastic filament to give it a strong connection.

The heart of hydra lies behind this rack and pinion component. Servos are screwed in upsidedown and the pinions are different length to account for the servo head offset. The gear box allows for optimum control over the fingers by translating rotational motion to linear.

The basic finger structure allows for mechanical motion.

A cool way to attach the hand to the forearm and give the thumb the great motion we desired there is an independent loft from the base of the palm to the forearm. Half of the servo is connected to the hand and half to the forearm for extra support.

The Actuator is a critical part of the mechanical motion of the fingers, the are all connected to the pinions, which lead into the gears and rack where the servos give control.
